The Brief

After completing onboarding, one of my first contributions was designing and building a custom enclosure for an embedded system using a Raspberry Pi Pico and a 900 MHz LoRa module.

I started by manually measuring the board with calipers, cross-referencing online specifications, and calculating the spacing and tolerances needed for a proper fit. I then designed the enclosure from scratch in Siemens NX and 3D printed the final case to make sure everything integrated cleanly.

Taking something from physical measurements to a functional printed component was a rewarding step into real hardware development.

Quick Notes

  • - Measured the board by hand with calipers
  • - Checked specs and fit tolerances against online references
  • - Designed the full enclosure from scratch in Siemens NX
  • - 3D printed the final PLA case for fit validation
