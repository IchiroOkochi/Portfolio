About

I am a Computer Engineering student at Purdue University building intelligent systems where artificial intelligence, software, and hardware meet — with a focus on turning ideas into real-world technology.

My work explores the intersection of machine learning, embedded systems, and real-world engineering. I am particularly interested in systems where intelligence leaves the screen and interacts with the physical world from AI-powered applications to flight hardware.

Currently, I contribute to the Purdue Space Program's Avionics Team, designing and developing hardware for high-power rockets, while also serving as a Design Lead in Purdue's Engineering Projects in Community Service (EPICS) program, where I help build AI-driven tools aimed at solving real-world problems.

I'm driven by the belief that the most powerful engineering is often invisible — systems that are intelligent, reliable, and quietly shape the world around them.
